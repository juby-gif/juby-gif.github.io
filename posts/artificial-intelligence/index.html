Artificial Intelligence on Health care

Well, before jumping into the actual topic we should understand what an AI is and we’re going to see that next.

What is Artificial Intelligence ?

Artificial Intelligence is the technology that is developed on stimulating human intelligence on smart machines or robots which are programmatically capable of replicating human actions and interactions. Ideally, it is based on the principle that human intelligence can be defined in a way that a machine can easily adapt, replicate it and execute tasks, from the most simple to those that are even more complex. The goals of artificial intelligence include learning, reasoning, and perception. AI techniques includes Machine learning and deep learning.

Why AI is getting so popular ?

As the world is leaning towards fully automation, it’s not surprising when the Artificial Intelligence get’s the priority. The human dependancy on machines had exponentially increased a decade ago. As a result, the science is leaning towards human intelligence to be mimiced over machines.

According to Towards Data Science, the major reasons for AI’s popularity was:

  • Higher computing power
  • Higher data access
  • Efficient Algorithms
  • Higher Investments

Latest trends of AI in Health care ?

The AI had a very high volume of potential applications throughout the different sectors and one which is more in demand is the healthcare. This will make a revolutionary impact on consumer lives as it puts them in control of their health and well-being. It can provide more accurate feedbacks to the consumers when they’re in need and ensures their health and safety.

Here are the latest AI products that are getting ready to conquer the market:

  • ONE DROP AI The One Drop AI will measure the consumer’s real-time data and compare it with the past users data points which will be generated as a forecast and insights into the current moment. As a result, the consumer can predict what’s going to happen next and get proper advice and feedbacks to be taken in those situations.

  • PATHAI

    The PATHAI is a developing AI technology by feeding the machine models with the rich data patterns of cancer that the pathologists see and thereby diagnose the patient with more precision and reduced errors.
